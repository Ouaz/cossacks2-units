Chasseur

Fast soldier, weak in hand-to-hand combat, good shot, tireless, good for killing enemy officers, heavy coal consumption when shooting.

The word "chasseur" itself means "hunter" in French, which deliberately describes how these troops have been founded and what their way of warfare is.

Being very mobile, they do not endure fatigue during long marches even while moving cross-country.

They are few in number-per-squad but have a great firepower, which actually makes them ideal skirmishers to slay the enemy troops' officer staffs.
