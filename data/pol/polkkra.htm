Mounted Krakuse

High melee damage, pistols (double-shot), effectively demoralizes enemy in melee, increased level of health.

During the Russian campaign, Napoleon felt the lack of maneuverable irregular cavalry, capable to perform audacious raids and competent enough to repulse the Russian Cossacks and save the carts from their barbaric forays.

The Emperor issued an order to establish a detachment of "Polish Cossacks" in the Duchy of Warsaw. The detachment was created and all its soldiers wore headdresses - "krakuses". Moreover, each of the other uniform parts was typical for the Krakow dwellers.

High command had generally discredited the Polish riders until krakuses utterly defeated their main enemy - the Cossacks - at the village of Ebersbach.
